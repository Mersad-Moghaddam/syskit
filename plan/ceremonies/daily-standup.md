# Ceremony: Daily Standup

> **When:** every working day · **Timebox:** 15 minutes (or async) · **Output:** blockers surfaced, board current

---

## Purpose

Keep the team synchronized and surface blockers **early**. The standup is for the Developers to coordinate toward the sprint goal — it is not a status report to a manager.

## Format

Each developer answers three questions:

1. **Yesterday** — what did I move toward Done?
2. **Today** — what will I move toward Done?
3. **Blockers** — what is stopping me?

## Async-friendly for open source

Because contributors are distributed and part-time, a **written daily update** in the sprint channel satisfies the standup. Post the three answers; the Scrum Master scans for blockers and dependencies. A live call happens only when a blocker needs real-time resolution.

## Rules that keep it to 15 minutes

- **Talk to the board, not the room.** Walk the sprint board right-to-left (closest-to-Done first) so nearly-finished work gets attention.
- **Blockers are named, not solved.** Detailed problem-solving happens *after* standup with only the people involved ("take it offline").
- **Respect the WIP limit.** If someone is blocked, the priority is unblocking them before anyone starts new work.
- **One conversation at a time.**

## What the Scrum Master does with it

- Records blockers in the impediment log and drives them (a blocker is the SM's top priority).
- Watches the **burndown**: a flat line past mid-sprint means stories aren't reaching Done — investigate.
- Escalates cross-cutting risks into `../risk-register.md`.

## Anti-patterns

- Turning it into a design meeting (take it offline).
- Reporting activity ("I worked on cpu stuff") instead of progress toward Done.
- Skipping it when things feel fine — that's exactly when small blockers hide.
